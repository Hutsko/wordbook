* A recent study that I find fascinating: Take a bunch of jocks, of both genders, let them compete at their sport, and you’ll find that they’ve developed stress-induced analgesia as a result (as measured by, say, their ability to keep their hand in a bucket of ice water for a longer period after the athletics than before). For the women, the key variable is the exercise, in that the analgesia will also be produced by time on an exercise bike. In contrast, for the men, the key variable is the competition, in that the analgesia is also induced by a competitive video game.
